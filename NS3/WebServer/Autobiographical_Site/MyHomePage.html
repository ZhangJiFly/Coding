About Me

First off I grew up in the country side of essex; swimming, climbing trees (which will come into greater discussion later), throwing rocks that sort of thing. I come from a really rather eccentric family of 3 brothers.

I seem to have a fairly periodically addictive personality, i have had mild obsessions with, Cadbury's chocolate mini-rolls, diet coke, caledonian sparkling water, and a rather more substantial obsession with watching large quantities of television programs in DVD form. I am amazingly apt at doing absolutely nothing without feeling boredom a talent some might say means i waste a large amount of time in fact doing nothing, but i still feel it is a talent rather than a curse.

Now for the slightly more interesting aspect of myself, On the 25th June 2007 3 days after my final GCSE exam, I fell from a 60ft tree obtaining my very first SCI & burst fracture of my T12 vertebra yeah boi! leaving me paralysed, due to damage to my spinal cord at L3. My surgeon informed me (well my dad, I was actually kept in the dark for a while) that I would have to stay for 4 - 6, but booyah to him it took me just 8 weeks (thats right because I am that awesome). On arrival at the Royal National Orthopedic Hospital (who were the only people who didn't either say your too broken or your too young), I could feel pretty much nothing below the waste and had very slight movement of the legs. After coming out of "Spinal Shock" I had far greater movement and feeling, and I can now stand as long as i have something to lean on, and walk with crutches or just a wall.

On the 25th of June 2008 I re-climbed this tree more or less just because I could, OK it was not quite the climb I had planned as most of it was more or less just tugging on rope, which is so not the same. On the other-hand the hardest part of the climb was free climb (but at the bottom of the tree so not that dangerous).

The broken spine left my GCSE summer of love with something more to be desired, not that I didn't actually quite enjoy my time in hospital I got to spend a lot of time in bed watching DVD's result! I got told quite how awesome I was on a fairly daily basis and I got to shout abuse at the nurses all day. It just was not the summer I had planned of sun bathing by the pool, climbing trees, drinking and general merriment. None-the-less come september I was there first day of school, taking my 3 A levels: physics, maths and economics.
